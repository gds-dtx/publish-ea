    Data & Analytics

    About this domain

    Managing, governing, and extracting insight from council data to support decision-making and statutory obligations. Covers master data management, business intelligence and reporting, data warehousing, data quality and governance, open data publishing, and geospatial platforms.

    Data and analytics capabilities sit in the Enabling Technology layer of the Local Government Architecture Model. They provide the intelligence layer that allows councils to understand demand, measure performance, meet statutory reporting obligations, and make evidence-based decisions.

    Why this matters for councils

    Understanding the local government context for data and analytics decisions:

    • Siloed data across many systems — councils hold vast amounts of citizen data across siloed systems but struggle to get a unified view of residents and services.
    • Statutory reporting obligations — ASC-CLD, ASC-FR, SAE, school census, housing returns, and other statutory returns require reliable, consistent data extraction from operational systems.
    • Evidence-based decision making — member scrutiny and cabinet decisions increasingly expect evidence-based analysis. Officers need self-service access to performance data.
    • Poor data quality — duplicate records, inconsistent addresses, outdated information are common because there is no master data strategy. This undermines every downstream use.
    • Open data obligations — the Transparency Code, Freedom of Information (FOI), and planning data requirements mean councils must actively publish datasets, not just respond to requests.
    • Geospatial complexity — Geographic Information System (GIS), Local Land and Property Gazetteer (LLPG), planning boundaries, and location intelligence underpin many council services but are often managed in isolation from other data.
    • GDPR/DPA compliance — General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)/Data Protection Act (DPA) compliance requires knowing what data you hold, where it is, and how long you keep it — active governance that most councils lack.

    Key capabilities

    The core data and analytics capabilities that councils need to manage their information assets effectively.

    Master Data Management

    Maintaining single authoritative records for citizens, properties (LLPG/National Land and Property Gazetteer (NLPG)), addresses, and council assets. The foundation for cross-service working — without clean master data, integration and analytics are unreliable.

    Business Intelligence & Reporting

    Dashboards, statutory returns, performance scorecards, and ad-hoc analytics for officers and elected members. Typically Power BI, but some councils use Qlik, Tableau, or bespoke tools.

    Data Warehouse

    Central analytical data store enabling cross-service analysis. Breaks down departmental silos by bringing data from multiple systems into one place for reporting and insight.

    Data Quality & Governance

    Data standards, lineage tracking, cataloguing, stewardship, retention schedules, and quality rules. Ensures data is accurate, consistent, and compliant. Often the weakest capability in councils.

    Open Data Publishing

    Publishing council datasets for transparency, FOI compliance, and reuse by citizens, businesses, and researchers. Required under the Local Government Transparency Code.

    Geospatial Platform

    GIS, mapping services, spatial analysis, Ordnance Survey data, LLPG management. Location intelligence underpins planning, highways, environmental services, and asset management.

    What to consider

    Key questions when assessing data and analytics capabilities:

    • Do you have a single, authoritative source for citizen and property records — or do multiple systems hold conflicting versions?
    • Can you produce statutory returns (ASC-CLD, council tax base, housing stats) without manual data manipulation?
    • Do elected members and senior officers have access to self-service analytics, or does every question require an ad-hoc report from a specialist?
    • How do you manage data retention — can you confidently say what data you hold and why?
    • Are your GIS and address data (LLPG) integrated with your service systems, or maintained separately?
    • Do you publish open data proactively, or only reactively in response to FOI requests?

    Available platforms & tools

    GDS / Public Sector

    Data Marketplace — for government organisations to find, request and share data across the public sector.

    Find Open Data — national open data portal for publishing and discovering government datasets.

    planning.data.gov.uk — planning datasets published by MHCLG, standardising local authority planning data.

    GeoPlace — national address and street data management (LLPG/NLPG), the definitive source for property records.

    Commercial (commonly used by councils)

    • Microsoft Power BI — dominant Business Intelligence (BI) tool across local government (included in M365 licensing).
    • Microsoft Azure Synapse / SQL Data Warehouse — cloud data warehousing for councils on the Microsoft ecosystem.
    • Qlik / Tableau — alternative BI platforms used where councils have existing investment or specific requirements.
    • Informatica / Talend — data quality and integration platforms for master data management.
    • ESRI ArcGIS / QGIS — geospatial platforms for mapping, spatial analysis, and location intelligence.
    • Socrata (Tyler Technologies) — open data publishing platform for transparency compliance.
    • LG Inform — LGA benchmarking platform where councils submit and compare performance data.

    Standards & guidance

    Relevant standards and guidance for data and analytics decisions:

    • Metadata standards for sharing and publishing data — Data Standards Authority guidance
    • Data Maturity Assessment for government — self-assessment framework for data capability
    • Managing Geospatial Data — FAIR data principles for location data
    • Data Ownership in Government — best practice on data stewardship
    • Data Standards Authority — collaboration space for government data standards
    • Local Government Transparency Code — requirements for open data publication by councils

    Commercial frameworks

    Crown Commercial Service (CCS) agreements relevant to data and analytics procurement:

    • Big Data and Analytics — intelligent data and analytics services
    • Records Information Management — digital records solutions
    • Technology Services — including data management services

    Connections to service delivery

    Data & Analytics enables the entire LGAM service delivery stack:

    • All Business Areas — statutory reporting (ASC-CLD, school census, housing returns) depends on governed data extraction
    • Workflow — case management benefits from pre-populated data and cross-service intelligence
    • Corporate CRM — relies on master citizen and property records for a single view of the resident
    • IT Systems — data warehouse integrates data from across all line-of-business systems
    • Planning & Development — geospatial platform underpins planning applications, land charges, and local plan evidence
