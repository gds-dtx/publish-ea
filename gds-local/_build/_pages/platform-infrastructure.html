    Platform & Infrastructure

    About this domain

    Core technology services, platforms, and operations that provide the essential foundations for all council digital systems. Covers cloud hosting, networking, end-user computing, identity and access management, service management, monitoring, and disaster recovery.

    Platform and infrastructure capabilities sit in the Enabling Technology layer of the Local Government Architecture Model. They underpin every other layer — without reliable hosting, connectivity, and operational tooling, no digital service can function.

    Why this matters for councils

    Understanding the local government context for platform and infrastructure decisions:

    • Small organisations with big responsibilities — most of England's 300+ councils are small organisations. They typically outsource or share infrastructure rather than running dedicated platform teams.
    • PSN wind-down — the Public Services Network (PSN) is being decommissioned. Councils are transitioning to internet-first connectivity with zero-trust security models and Software-Defined Wide Area Network (SD-WAN).
    • Two-tier complexity — in two-tier areas, county councils often provide shared infrastructure (Wide Area Network (WAN), hosting, directory services) that district councils consume. Disaggregation creates risk when relationships change.
    • End-user computing costs — device refresh cycles, hybrid working support, and mobile device management represent a major ongoing expenditure for all councils.
    • Disaster recovery gaps — Disaster Recovery (DR) is often neglected until an incident occurs. High-profile cyber attacks on councils (Hackney, Redcar & Cleveland, Gloucester) have highlighted the critical importance of tested recovery capabilities.

    Key capabilities

    The core platform and infrastructure capabilities that councils need to operate their technology estate.

    Cloud Hosting

    Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS) and Platform as a Service (PaaS) for running council applications. Public cloud, private cloud, hybrid, and community cloud arrangements. Most councils use Azure or AWS, with some legacy on-premises hosting for specialist systems.

    Network & Connectivity

    WAN, Local Area Network (LAN), SD-WAN, internet breakout, Wi-Fi, and inter-site links. Councils with multiple offices, libraries, and depots need reliable, secure connectivity. Transitioning from PSN to internet-first with zero-trust.

    End-User Computing

    Desktops, laptops, mobile devices, thin clients, and Virtual Desktop Infrastructure (VDI). The tools officers and social workers use daily. Includes device management (Intune/SCCM), patching, and hybrid working support.

    Identity & Access Management

    Staff Single Sign-On (SSO), Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA), directory services (Entra ID/Active Directory), privileged access management, role-based access control. Distinct from citizen-facing identity (which is in the Capabilities layer).

    Service Management (ITSM)

    Incident, problem, change, configuration, and service request management. How the council operates its technology estate. Often delivered via ServiceNow, Freshservice, or Hornbill.

    Monitoring & Observability

    Infrastructure and application monitoring, alerting, log aggregation, performance baselining. Essential for proactive management and meeting SLAs.

    Disaster Recovery & Business Continuity

    Backup, replication, failover, DR testing, and Recovery Time Objective (RTO)/Recovery Point Objective (RPO) targets for critical systems. Councils must maintain service continuity for statutory functions.

    What to consider

    Key questions when assessing platform and infrastructure capabilities:

    • Do you have a clear cloud strategy (cloud-first, hybrid, or specific workload placement)?
    • How do you manage the lifecycle of end-user devices, and does your refresh cycle keep pace with security requirements?
    • Is your network architecture ready for internet-first (post-PSN) connectivity?
    • Can you recover critical systems within your stated RTO/RPO in the event of ransomware or data centre failure?
    • How is privileged access managed — do you have separation between admin and standard accounts?
    • What is your approach to multi-site connectivity — SD-WAN, MPLS, or internet VPN?

    Available platforms & tools

    GDS / Public Sector

    GovWifi — shared wifi authentication service for public sector buildings. Allows staff and visitors to connect to a trusted network across government offices, council buildings, and libraries.

    GOV.UK PaaS — note: decommissioned in 2023. Councils should use commercial cloud platforms directly (Azure, AWS) rather than expecting a central government hosting service.

    Commercial (commonly used by councils)

    • Microsoft Azure / Microsoft 365 — dominant cloud and productivity platform across local government. Most councils standardise on the Microsoft ecosystem for infrastructure, identity, and collaboration.
    • Amazon Web Services (AWS) — used by some councils, particularly for specific workloads or where vendor diversity is preferred.
    • VMware (Broadcom) — virtualisation platform for councils retaining on-premises infrastructure or running private cloud environments.
    • ServiceNow / Freshservice / Hornbill — IT Service Management (ITSM) platforms for managing incidents, service requests, changes, and the configuration management database.
    • Microsoft Intune / SCCM — device management platforms for deploying, configuring, and securing end-user computing devices.

    Standards & guidance

    Relevant standards and guidance for platform and infrastructure decisions:

    • Cloud guidance for the public sector — resources for using cloud platforms in public sector organisations
    • Microsoft 365 guidance for UK government — recommended configurations for M365
    • Minimum Cyber Security Standard — baseline security expectations for public sector organisations

    Commercial frameworks

    Crown Commercial Service (CCS) agreements relevant to platform and infrastructure procurement:

    • Cloud and Hosting — cloud hosting and managed services
    • Network Services — WAN, LAN, and connectivity services
    • Mobile Voice and Data — mobile devices and data contracts
    • Technology Products — end-user devices, servers, storage, accessories
    • Technology Services — IT service support and managed services
    • MFD Services — print, hardware, and digital workflow

    Connections to service delivery

    Platform & Infrastructure enables the entire LGAM service delivery stack:

    • All IT Systems (CRM, Social Care, Planning, etc.) run on cloud hosting or on-premises infrastructure
    • Council Interfaces (Website, Apps, API Gateway) depend on network connectivity and hosting
    • Service Management underpins the operational reliability of all capabilities
    • Identity & Access Management secures staff access to back-office systems
