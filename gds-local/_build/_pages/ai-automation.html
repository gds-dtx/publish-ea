    AI & Automation

    About this domain

    Platforms and governance for automating internal processes, predictive analytics, and responsible AI deployment. Covers Robotic Process Automation (RPA), machine learning and predictive analytics, intelligent document processing, process mining, AI governance and ethics, and conversational AI platforms.

    Why this matters for councils

    Understanding the local government context for AI and automation decisions:

    • Demand vs. budget pressure — councils face increasing demand with shrinking budgets — automation is essential for maintaining service levels.
    • RPA maturity — RPA is the most established automation technology in local government, often used to work around poor vendor APIs and manual data entry between systems.
    • AI/ML is emerging — AI/Machine Learning (ML) is earlier in maturity — most councils are exploring or piloting rather than deploying at scale.
    • Conversational AI — conversational AI (chatbots) is increasingly used for citizen-facing interactions (council websites, phone lines) — it underpins the LGAM "Agentic AI" capability in the service delivery layer.
    • Document processing — document processing automation is high-value for councils dealing with planning applications, benefits claims, correspondence, and Freedom of Information (FOI) requests.
    • Democratic accountability — democratic accountability means algorithmic decision-making needs governance, transparency, and public scrutiny (Algorithmic Transparency Recording Standard (ATRS) requirements).
    • Process discovery — process mining helps councils discover where automation candidates exist before investing in solutions.

    Key capabilities

    The core AI and automation capabilities that councils need to deliver efficient, accountable services.

    Robotic Process Automation (RPA)

    Automating repetitive back-office tasks: data entry between systems, reconciliation, report generation, system-to-system transfers where APIs don't exist. Most councils start their automation journey here. Tools: UiPath, Microsoft Power Automate, Blue Prism.

    Machine Learning & Predictive Analytics

    Training and deploying models for demand forecasting, early intervention scoring (children's services, adult social care), fraud detection, and resource optimisation. Requires data maturity and specialist skills.

    Intelligent Document Processing

    Automated extraction, classification, and validation of data from unstructured documents: planning applications, benefit claims, correspondence, invoices. Reduces manual data entry and processing time.

    Process Mining

    Discovering actual process flows from system event logs. Identifies bottlenecks, compliance deviations, and automation candidates. Helps councils understand how work actually flows before redesigning it.

    AI Governance & Ethics

    Algorithmic impact assessments, bias monitoring, model registers, explainability requirements, and public transparency obligations (ATRS). Councils have democratic accountability for automated decisions affecting citizens.

    Conversational AI Platform

    Infrastructure for building, training, and deploying chatbots and virtual agents. Underpins the LGAM service delivery layer's "Agentic AI" capability. Includes Natural Language Understanding (NLU), dialogue management, and channel integration.

    What to consider

    Key questions when assessing AI and automation capabilities:

    • Are you starting with RPA (quick wins, no ML expertise needed) or looking at more advanced AI/ML?
    • Do you have the data maturity to support predictive analytics — clean, governed, accessible data?
    • How will you govern AI decision-making — who is accountable when an algorithm affects a citizen?
    • Are you meeting ATRS (Algorithmic Transparency Recording Standard) requirements for any automated decisions?
    • Do your existing chatbot/virtual agent tools have the capability for multi-step reasoning, or are they rule-based only?
    • Have you identified your highest-volume document types that could benefit from automated processing?
    • Is there a clear business case for each automation — or is it technology looking for a problem?

    Available platforms & tools

    GDS / Public Sector

    AI Incubator Tools — agile delivery function for AI tools and resources. Supports public sector organisations exploring AI adoption with practical guidance and tooling.

    Algorithmic Transparency Recording Standard (ATRS) — publication standard for algorithmic tools used in public sector decision-making. Required for councils deploying automated decisions affecting citizens.

    Commercial (commonly used by councils)

    • Microsoft Power Automate / Copilot — RPA and AI assistant bundled with M365. The most accessible starting point for councils already in the Microsoft ecosystem.
    • UiPath — enterprise RPA platform common in larger councils with established automation programmes.
    • Blue Prism — enterprise RPA platform with strong governance controls for regulated environments.
    • Microsoft Azure AI Services — ML, document intelligence, and cognitive services for councils building custom AI solutions.
    • Amazon Textract / AWS AI — document processing and ML services for councils on the AWS platform.
    • Google Dialogflow / Microsoft Bot Framework — conversational AI platforms for building chatbots and virtual agents.
    • Celonis / Microsoft Process Mining — process discovery tools that analyse event logs to identify automation opportunities.
    • Local government-specific — Jadu (chatbots), FutrLi, Sensemaker, and other tools designed for council use cases.

    Standards & guidance

    Relevant standards and guidance for AI and automation decisions:

    • AI Playbook for UK Government — guidance on using AI safely, effectively, and securely
    • Responsible AI Toolkit — practitioner toolkit for responsible AI use
    • Algorithmic Transparency Recording Standard — how public sector organisations publish information about algorithmic tools
    • Data Ethics Framework — framework for ethical data use in government

    Commercial frameworks

    Crown Commercial Service (CCS) agreements relevant to AI and automation procurement:

    • Artificial Intelligence — CCS agreement for AI discovery, consultancy, implementation, and access to AI technologies
    • SPARK - Emergent Technology — CCS agreement for emerging tech, IoT, AI and automation
    • Automation Marketplace — CCS agreement for automation solutions and services

    Connections to service delivery

    AI & Automation enables the LGAM service delivery stack:

    • Agentic AI (Capabilities layer) — conversational AI platform underpins citizen-facing AI agents
    • Workflow — process mining identifies improvement opportunities; RPA automates handoffs between stages
    • Forms — intelligent document processing automates extraction of data from submitted documents
    • All Business Areas — predictive analytics supports early intervention (social care), demand management (waste), and fraud detection (revenues & benefits)
